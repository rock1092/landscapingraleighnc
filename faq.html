Lawn Care & Maintenance

Raleigh sits in a transitional climate zone, but warm-season grasses tend to perform best in Wake County's hot summers. Your top options are:

Bermuda grass — heat-tolerant, drought-resistant, ideal for full-sun lawns. Goes dormant (brown) in winter.
Zoysia grass — dense, low-maintenance, holds color later into fall. Slower to establish but very durable.
Tall Fescue — the best option for shady yards. Stays green year-round but needs annual overseeding in fall.

We help you choose the right variety for your specific sun exposure, soil, and maintenance goals. See our sod installation page for more.
During peak growing season (April–October), most Raleigh lawns need mowing every 7–10 days. Bermuda grass in full sun may need weekly mowing during summer. During cooler months (November–March), bi-weekly or monthly mowing is typically sufficient. We offer weekly, bi-weekly, and custom scheduling plans. Learn more about our lawn care services.
The timing depends on your grass type:

Bermuda or Zoysia lawns: Aerate in late spring to early summer (May–June) when the grass is actively growing and can recover quickly.
Tall Fescue lawns: Aerate in early fall (September–October) just before or during overseeding season.

Aeration relieves Raleigh's notoriously heavy clay soil compaction, improves water and nutrient penetration, and makes fertilization significantly more effective.
Sod gives you an instant, established lawn — usable within 2–3 weeks. It's more expensive upfront but far more reliable. Seeding is less expensive but takes 6–12 weeks to fill in, requires intensive watering, and is more vulnerable to weeds and erosion during establishment.

For most Raleigh homeowners who want quick, reliable results, sod is the better choice. For large areas on a tighter budget, fall seeding with Tall Fescue is cost-effective. See our sod installation page for pricing and details.
Fall (September–November) is the best time to plant most trees and shrubs in Raleigh. Cooler temperatures reduce transplant stress, and roots continue establishing through winter so plants are ready to thrive come spring. Spring planting (March–April) is the second-best window. Avoid planting during Raleigh's hottest summer months unless you're prepared for intensive daily watering.

Drainage & Irrigation

Standing water in your yard is usually caused by one of four issues:

1. Improper grading — your yard slopes toward the house instead of away from it.
2. Compacted clay soil — extremely common in Raleigh's heavy clay, water can't absorb fast enough.
3. Low spots — natural depressions that collect water with nowhere to drain.
4. Inadequate drainage infrastructure — no system to carry water off the property.

Solutions include French drains, yard regrading, dry creek beds, or channel drains. We assess drainage issues free of charge as part of our estimate. Learn more about drainage solutions.
A French drain is a gravel-filled trench with a perforated pipe that redirects groundwater and surface water away from problem areas. You likely need one if:

• You have persistent wet spots that don't dry within 24–48 hours after rain
• Water is pooling near your home's foundation
• You have soil erosion on slopes
• Certain areas of your yard stay soggy through the growing season

French drains are one of the most effective long-term solutions for Raleigh's heavy clay drainage issues. See our drainage page.

Hardscaping & Mulch

The standard recommendation is a 2–3 inch layer of fresh mulch. To estimate your needs: measure your bed area in square feet, then divide by 100 to get approximate cubic yards for a 3-inch layer. A 500 sq ft bed needs about 5 cubic yards.
